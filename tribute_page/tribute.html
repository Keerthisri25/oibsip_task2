Elvis Presley

King of Rock and Roll

Elvis Aaron Presley, also known as the "King of Rock and Roll," was an American singer, musician, and actor. He was born on January 8, 1935, in Tupelo, Mississippi, and rose to fame in the mid-1950s with his distinctive voice and energetic performances.

Some of his most famous songs include "Heartbreak Hotel," "Jailhouse Rock," and "Love Me Tender." Elvis became a cultural icon and a symbol of the rock and roll revolution. His influence on music and popular culture is still felt today.

Elvis Presley passed away on August 16, 1977, but his music and legacy continue to live on, making him one of the most celebrated and enduring figures in the history of music.

Some of his achievements:

Having sold about 500 million records worldwide, Presley is one of the best-selling music artists of all time.

Presley holds the records for most songs charting in Billboard's top 40 and top 100

According to Billboard, Presley has 79 cumulative weeks at number one: alone at 80, according to Whitburn and the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, with only Mariah Carey having more with 91 weeks.

He holds the records for most number-one singles on the UK chart with 21 and singles reaching the top ten with 76.

As an album artist, Presley is credited by Billboard with the record for the most albums charting in the Billboard 200: 129, far ahead of second-place Frank Sinatra's 82.

He also holds the record for most time spent at number one on the Billboard 200: 67 weeks.
